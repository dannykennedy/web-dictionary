How to use this dictionary

  • The search bar looks for both Bininj Kunwok and English terms as well as dialect variations. Click on a word to open the full entry.
  • The toggle allows you to switch between regular Bininj Kunwok and Kunbalak - a special register used with one's mother-in-law (actual and classificatory) as well as when talking to her brothers. It's often described as 'Mother-in-law language')
  • The dictionary is currently weighted towards Kunwinjku as spoken at Kunbarlanja, so if you're learning a different dialect, keep an eye on the 'other languages' section within certain entries.
  • It's still a work in progress, and in all questions of correctness and appropriate use you should defer to native speakers.
  • You can't always find a direct translation for words, and different words have different ranges of meaning Bininj Kunwok and English. The Kunwinjku textbook available here is a great resource to complement this dictionary.
  • Another great resource is the Kundjeyhmi Dictionary Android app, a sister project of this app developed by Corey Spink. Kundjeyhmi is another dialect of Bininj Kunwok and if you can't find the word here, it might be there!
  • Check out the Karriyolyolme YouTube channel!
  • Phrasebook (beta!)
